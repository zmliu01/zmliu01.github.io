      <h2 id="about-me">About Me</h2>

<p>Hi! My name is Xing Chen (陈醒). I am an assistant professor at <a href="https://sirpa.fudan.edu.cn/">School of International Relations and Public Affairs</a> at <a href="https://www.fudan.edu.cn/en/">Fudan University</a>. I obtained my PhD in Environmental Economics and bachelor degree in Environmental Science from <a href="https://english.pku.edu.cn/">Peking University</a>. My advisor was Professor <a href="https://www.nsd.pku.edu.cn/szdw/qzjs/x/262188.htm#">Jintao Xu</a> from <a href="https://www.nsd.pku.edu.cn/xygk/xygk1/index.htm">National School of Development</a>. Additionally, I spent a year as a visiting scholar at <a href="https://are.berkeley.edu/">UC Berkeley</a>, supervised by the esteemed environmental economist, <a href="https://are.berkeley.edu/users/peter-berck">Peter Berck</a>.</p>

<p>My experience at Shanghai ignited my interest in politics and diverted me from my initial academic path. I now study <strong>Political Economy, Public Opinion, and Political Psychology</strong>. Leveraging observations from the real world, I provide evidence-based insights into how information, trauma, and cultural norms shape political trust and decision-making. My research connects public administration, political science, and economics, with work published in  <strong>Governance</strong>, <strong>Public Performance&amp;Management Review</strong>. If you want to collaborate, feel free to email me.</p>
